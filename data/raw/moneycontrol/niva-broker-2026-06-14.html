Iran threatens 'strong response' to Beirut strike as Trump says US-Iran deal remains on track

A senior Iranian lawmaker has threatened retaliation following Israel's deadly strike on Beirut's southern suburbs, as US President Donald Trump insisted that negotiations with Tehran remained on track despite the latest escalation.

Ebrahim Azizi, head of the National Security Committee in Iran's parliament, said the attack on Dahiyeh, a Hezbollah stronghold in southern Beirut, demonstrated Washington's inability to restrain its ally.

“Today’s crime by the Zionist regime in Dahieh, Beirut once again proved the US is weak without credibility, as it is not even capable of controlling this illegitimate regime.

“A strong response is coming.”

Azizi made the remarks in a post on X after the Israeli strike in Beirut, where Iran-backed Hezbollah holds significant influence.

Despite Tehran's warning, Trump sought to calm tensions and said he would ask Iran not to retaliate against Israel, according to Fox News.

The US president also told Axios that negotiations aimed at ending the conflict and securing an agreement with Iran were still expected to proceed on Sunday, although the Beirut strike had disrupted the timetable.

“[The Israeli attack] shook it up. It delayed the signing by a few hours. It was supposed to be now. Now it is scheduled for a few hours from now,” Trump said in a phone conversation with Axios.

Trump said he had been taken aback when advisers informed him of the strike and expressed frustration with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu.

“It is so bad – I couldn’t believe it. An hour before we are supposed to sign the deal,” Axios quoted the leader as saying.

Trump argued that the proposed agreement would benefit Israel by preventing Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon, ensuring the disposal of nuclear material and allowing snap inspections of Iranian facilities.
